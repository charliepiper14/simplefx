Pain Point Research: What We Learned

We analyzed the emotional and practical pain points driving people to search for forex education. This informs our ad copy, landing page messaging, and keyword targeting.

Life-Altering & Urgent Pains

Fear of Financial Loss

  • "I've lost a large chunk of my savings because I tried to trade without proper guidance"
  • "What if I blow my account and have to go back to relying on someone else's paycheque"
  • How We Address: Emphasize risk management training, free education to test fit before investing, proven track record since 2010

Fear of Failure

  • "If I don't become profitable soon, this will just be another failed side project"
  • "I told myself I'd use trading as the ticket out of the rat race, but I'm more stressed than ever"
    • How We Address: Highlight structured learning path, mentorship support, realistic expectations setting

Fear of Inadequacy

  • "Everyone else knows how to read charts and I'm clueless"
  • "I feel like an imposter when I open the trading platform"
  • How We Address: Beginner-friendly messaging, "Is Forex For Me" entry point, no judgment language

Uncertainty & Overwhelm

  • "There are so many courses and hype, how can I trust one"
  • "One guru says one thing, another says the opposite — who do I trust"
  • How We Address: Awards, 15+ years experience, free trial events, transparent approach

Key Takeaway for Ad Copy

  • Lead with problem awareness (the pain they're feeling right now)
  • Address the fear of wasting money on yet another course that doesn't deliver
  • Emphasize credibility markers: 15+ years, award-winning, structured path
  • Use risk-reversal: Free events to test fit before committing
  • Speak to aspiration: Freedom, financial independence, second income

Landing Page & Funnel Strategy

Search traffic has different expectations than social traffic. Here's how we adapt the funnel for Google Ads visitors.

Option 1: Direct to Event Registration (Recommended for Launch)

  • Pro: Shortest path to conversion, mirrors Meta setup
  • Pro: Consistent experience across channels
  • Pro: Easy to set up and track
  • Con: May miss opportunity to educate searchers who need more context
  • Best For: High-intent keywords like "forex trading course"

Option 2: Dedicated Google Ads Landing Page (Test After Launch)

  • Pro: Can tailor messaging to search intent (pain points they searched for)
  • Pro: Build credibility before asking for registration
  • Pro: Can segment messaging by keyword (beginners vs frustrated traders)
  • Con: Extra step in funnel = potential drop-off
  • Best For: Broad keywords like "learn forex trading"

Recommended Approach

  • Week 1-2: Send all traffic directly to Zoom registration page (same as Meta). Keep it simple for launch.
  • Week 3-4: Once we have baseline data, build dedicated Google Ads landing page with dynamic content based on keyword groups
  • Week 5+: A/B test direct registration vs intermediate landing page to see which converts better
  • Critical: Ensure UTM parameters are set up properly to track which keywords drive registrations and which drive sales

Tracking & Conversion Setup

What Needs to Be Implemented

  1. Google Ads Conversion Tracking: Fire pixel when someone completes Zoom registration
  2. Google Analytics 4: Link to Google Ads account for deeper insights
  3. UTM Parameters: Track source (Google), medium (cpc), campaign name, and keyword
  4. Offline Conversion Import: When someone buys a course, upload that conversion back to Google Ads with the original click ID
  5. Call Tracking (Optional): If you get phone calls from ads, use call tracking number

Launch Checklist & Next Steps

Pre-Launch (This Week)

  1. Get final approval on budget (£53/day starting)
  2. Confirm Google Ads account access or create new account
  3. Set up conversion tracking on Zoom registration page
  4. Link Google Analytics 4 to Google Ads
  5. Add credit card to Google Ads account
  6. Review and approve ad copy examples

Week 1-2 (Learning Phase)

  1. Launch all 4 campaigns with conservative bids
  2. Monitor daily for disapprovals or policy issues
  3. Track which keywords drive clicks (but don't optimize yet)
  4. Let Google's algorithm gather data
  5. Weekly check-in on spend pacing and initial lead quality

Week 3-4 (Optimization Phase)

  1. Pause keywords with 0 conversions and high spend
  2. Increase bids on top performers
  3. Add negative keywords based on search term report
  4. Test new ad copy variations
  5. Begin scaling budget on winning campaigns
  6. Consider building dedicated landing page

Month 2+ (Scaling Phase)

  1. Scale daily budget to £70-85 on proven campaigns
  2. Launch remarketing campaigns for site visitors
  3. Test display campaigns for awareness
  4. Implement offline conversion tracking for course sales
  5. Expand keyword list based on winning themes
  6. Monthly performance review and strategy refinement
